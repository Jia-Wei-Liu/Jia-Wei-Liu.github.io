About ME

Jiawei Liu (刘佳玮)

jiawei.liu@u.nus.edu Google Scholar Linkedin Github

Hello! I am a Research Scientist at the SAM Team at Meta Superintelligence Labs, formerly FAIR Perception. I obtained my PhD in Computer Science from National University of Singapore, advised by Prof. Mike Zheng Shou and Prof. Jussi Keppo. I obtained my Master's degree from Tsinghua University.
My research focuses on building generalist intelligence for the physical world that can perceive, predict, and act in real-world environments.
I pursue this goal through both vision and language foundation models. On the vision side, I have been working on multi-modal 3D/4D/video generation and perception. On the language side, I focus on enabling frontier LLMs with physical intelligence through mid- and post-training for coding capabilities.

Selected Publications

SAM 3D: 3Dfy Anything in Images

SAM 3D Team (Contributed model mid-training and post-training).
IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR) 2026 Oral Presentation
Paper Project Demo Code

A 1.8B flow matching model (6T training tokens) for single-image 3D generation, that predicts complete shape, pose, and texture for arbitrary masked objects. Contributed mid-training for layout model and post-training for geometry model. Created 4.5M high-quality and semantically-rich 3D layout data through physical simulation, and 130K high-fidelity hand shape data. Designed layout post-optimization method (+8.7% 3D IoU, +26.1% 2D IoU).

SAM 3D Body: Robust Full-Body Human Mesh Recovery

SAM 3D Body Team (Led SAM3D unification, qualitative model evaluation, and public release).
IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR) 2026 Oral Presentation
Paper Project Demo Code

A promptable model for full-body single-image 3D human mesh recovery (HMR), that estimates complete pose and shape of the body, feet, and hands for arbitrary masked humans. Led SAM 3D unification, qualitative model evaluation, and user preference study. Drove end-to-end system release, including repository (GitHub & Hugging Face), paper revision, and live demo deployment.
